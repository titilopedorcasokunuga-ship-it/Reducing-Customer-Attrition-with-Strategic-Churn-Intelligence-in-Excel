# Business Introduction

TeleConnect operates in a highly competitive telecommunications market where customer acquisition costs are significantly higher than customer retention costs. Sustained profitability depends heavily on reducing customer churn and increasing customer lifetime value (CLV).

In subscription-based telecom models, churn directly impacts:
- Revenue stability
- Operational forecasting
- Marketing spend efficiency
- Brand equity

This project analyzes customer churn behavior using Microsoft Excel and presents a structured dashboard designed for executive-level decision-making.

---

# Problem Statement

TeleConnect has experienced consistent customer attrition, impacting recurring revenue and profitability. However, the organization lacked:

- Clear visibility into churn drivers
- Segmented churn analysis by customer attributes
- Data-backed retention strategies
- Executive-ready reporting for strategic action

The business problem addressed in this project:

> **Identify the key drivers of customer churn and provide actionable, measurable recommendations to reduce churn rate and improve retention.**

---

## 1. Overall Customer Churn

- **Total Customers Retained:** 81.3%
- **Total Customers Churned:** 18.7%

### Insight

An 18.7% churn rate in telecom is considered materially high and signals structural retention issues.

If sustained annually, this churn rate significantly erodes revenue and increases dependency on costly acquisition campaigns.

### Business Risk

- High customer acquisition cost (CAC) replacement
- Revenue volatility
- Increased pressure on marketing budgets

---

## 2. Churn by Contract Type

| Contract Type   | Churn Rate |
|-----------------|------------|
| Month-to-Month  | 51.67%     |
| One-Year        | 18.93%     |
| Two-Year        | 10.73%     |

### Insight

Customers on **month-to-month contracts churn at a dramatically higher rate (51.67%)** compared to long-term contracts.

This indicates:
- Low switching barriers
- Lack of contractual commitment
- Potential dissatisfaction or price sensitivity

### Business Interpretation

Long-term contracts create behavioral lock-in and revenue predictability. Month-to-month customers represent the highest churn risk segment.

---

## 3. Technical Support Impact on Churn

| Technical Support | Retention Rate | Churn Rate |
|-------------------|---------------|------------|
| No Support        | 74.64%        | 25.36%     |
| With Support      | 85.32%        | 14.68%     |

### Insight

Customers without technical support are nearly **2x more likely to churn**.

### Business Interpretation

Access to support:
- Increases perceived service value
- Reduces frustration
- Improves trust
- Enhances customer experience

Technical support is not a cost center ‚Äî it is a retention driver.

---

## 4. Payment Method vs Churn

Observations:

- **Electronic Check users show highest churn**
- Bank transfer and credit card users show comparatively lower churn

### Insight

Electronic check users may:
- Be less digitally integrated
- Have lower service commitment
- Represent price-sensitive segments

### Business Interpretation

Auto-payment methods correlate with lower churn due to:
- Reduced payment friction
- Increased customer inertia
- Behavioral continuity

---

## 5. Customer Satisfaction vs Churn

Churn decreases significantly as satisfaction score increases.

Low satisfaction customers show disproportionate churn volume.

### Insight

Customer satisfaction is a leading indicator of churn.

Customers rating satisfaction low are high-risk and require proactive intervention.

### Business Interpretation

Customer experience directly drives retention. Satisfaction scoring should be integrated into churn prediction modeling.

---

## 6. Customer Service Calls vs Churn

Customers with higher numbers of service calls show increased churn probability.

Notably:
- Customers with 4+ calls show materially higher churn rates.

### Insight

Frequent service calls indicate unresolved issues and poor first-call resolution performance.

### Business Interpretation

Call frequency is a churn predictor.

Operational inefficiency in issue resolution increases churn risk.
